US Military Researchers connect computers with telephone wire.

The first message is sent on ARPANET from UCLA to the Stanford Research Institute. At this time ARPANET linked UCLA, UC Santa Barbara, Stanford and the University of UTAH.

Tim Berners Lee proposes the concept of HTML, which enables 2D user interfaces to be transmitted efficiently over the web.

Dial-up Internet, originally a business or academic tool, is expanded to the general public via American OnLine (AOL)

Marc Andreeson develops the Mosaic Web browser at the University of Champaign, Illinois.

Jerry Yang and David Filo launch Yahoo at Stanford University, Match.com is launched, and the term Internet is first defined by The Federal Networking Council.

Google launches, IPV6 is introduced to expand the available web addresses, Paxos Protocol for Distributed Systems is published.

"The Dot Com Bubble" Bursts

Wikipedia.org is founded, providing the first open contribution database, and Wordpress launches, providing an open sourced platform for content creation and publishing.

Facebook launches their initial platform "the Facebook". Twitter launches and Jack Dorsey tweets "just setting up my twttr"

As US Financial Markets crash amid speculation and high risk, Satoshi Nakomoto publishes the Bitcoin whitepaper, proposing an alternative option.

Social media companies, in particular Facebook and Twitter, are instrumental in political revolutions in nearly a dozen Arab and African countries.

Ethereum launches amid revelations of indescriminate privacy breaches by the NSA, offering an alternative to centralized software platforms.
